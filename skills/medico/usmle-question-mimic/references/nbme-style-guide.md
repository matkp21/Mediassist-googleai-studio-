# NBME / USMLE Item Writing Style Guide

## 1. Core Structure of a Clinical Vignette
Every question MUST follow this exact sequence:
1. **Patient Demographics & Chief Complaint:** Age, sex, and primary reason for visit (e.g., "A 45-year-old man comes to the emergency department because of...").
2. **History of Present Illness (HPI):** Duration of symptoms, nature of symptoms, provoking/palliating factors.
3. **Past Medical History & Medications:** Only include if relevant or as a plausible distractor.
4. **Physical Examination:** Vitals first (Temp, BP, HR, RR), followed by general appearance, then focused system-specific findings.
5. **Diagnostic Studies:** Labs, imaging, ECG findings.
6. **The Lead-in Question:** Must be a clear, single sentence (e.g., "Which of the following is the most likely diagnosis?", "Which of the following is the most appropriate next step in management?").

## 2. Rules for Distractors (Options A-E)
- Must be homogenous (all diagnoses, or all drug mechanics, or all next steps).
- Must be plausible. Do not use definitively impossible answers.
- Order options alphabetically or numerically to avoid pattern guessing.
- Do NOT use "All of the above" or "None of the above".

## 3. Explanation Requirements
- **Correct Answer:** Clearly state the diagnosis/teaching point and WHY the correct option is the best fit based on the vignette's specific clues.
- **Incorrect Answers:** For EVERY incorrect option, explain what condition that option represents and exactly which clues in the vignette rule it out.
