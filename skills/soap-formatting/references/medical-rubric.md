# Medical Documentation Rubric: SOAP Note Standards

## 1. Subjective (S)
* **Chief Complaint (CC):** Must be concise and preferably in the patient's own words (in quotes).
* **History of Present Illness (HPI):** Must clearly outline the chronological story of the CC. Use the OLD CARTS mnemonic (Onset, Location, Duration, Character, Alleviating/Aggravating factors, Radiation, Timing, Severity).
* **Medical History:** Complete Past Medical History (PMHx), Past Surgical History (PSHx), Medications (including OTC and supplements), Allergies (include specific reaction), Family History (FHx), and Social History (SHx - tobacco, alcohol, drug use, occupation, living situation).
* **Review of Systems (ROS):** Pertinent positives and negatives.

## 2. Objective (O)
* **Vital Signs:** BP, HR, RR, Temp, SpO2, BMI.
* **Physical Exam:** Grouped methodically by system (e.g., General, HEENT, CV, Resp, Abd, Neuro, Ext, Skin). Avoid vague terms like "normal"; use descriptive terms like "clear to auscultation bilaterally" or "regular rate and rhythm."
* **Data:** Pertinent laboratory results and imaging/diagnostic findings available at the time of the note.

## 3. Assessment (A)
* **Summary Statement:** A 1-2 sentence quick synthesis of the patient's presentation (e.g., "54M with hx of HTN and HLD presenting with acute onset crushing chest pain").
* **Differential Diagnosis / Problem List:** Numbered list of active problems, prioritized by severity. Include rationale/evidence from S and O supporting each diagnosis.

## 4. Plan (P)
* Corresponds directly to the numbered active problems in the Assessment.
* **Diagnostic Plan:** Labs, imaging, or procedures ordered.
* **Therapeutic Plan:** Medications (with specific dosing and route), interventions.
* **Patient Education/Counseling:** What was discussed with the patient.
* **Follow-up / Disposition:** When to return, refer to specialist, or admission orders.

## General Formatting Rules
* Professional tone, objective language.
* Strict medical terminology and approved abbreviations only.
* No identifying patient information (HIPAA compliant structures).
* Use clear bullet points and bolding for readability.
