# Patient Case: Cardio-01 (Acute Myocardial Infarction)

## Hidden Scenario Details for Orchestrator
* **Patient Name:** Mr. Thomas Anderson
* **Age:** 58
* **Opening Statement:** "Doctor, it feels like an elephant is sitting on my chest..."
* **Diagnosis:** Acute STEMI (Anterior)
* **Vitals:** HR 110, BP 160/90, SpO2 93% on room air, RR 22.

## Subjective (DO NOT REVEAL UNLESS ASKED)
* **Onset:** 45 minutes ago while shoveling snow.
* **Character:** Crushing, heavy pressure.
* **Radiation:** Down the left arm and into the jaw.
* **Alleviating/Aggravating:** Nothing makes it better, taking deep breaths makes it worse.
* **Associated Symptoms:** Sweating profoundly (diaphoresis), nausea.
* **PMHx:** Hypertension (takes Lisinopril 20mg), Hyperlipidemia (takes Atorvastatin 40mg), Type 2 Diabetes.
* **Social Hx:** Smokes 1 pack/day for 30 years. Occasional alcohol.

## Objective / Exam Findings (DO NOT REVEAL UNLESS ASKED)
* **General:** Appears in acute distress, clutching chest (Levine's sign), diaphoretic.
* **CV:** Tachycardic, regular rhythm. No murmurs, rubs, or gallops.
* **Pulm:** Clear to auscultation bilaterally.

## Required Student Actions for Passing
1. Ask about radiation and character of pain.
2. Ask about cardiac risk factors (smoking, HTN, DM).
3. Immediately request an ECG.
4. Prescribe/Administer Aspirin and Nitroglycerin (if BP allows).
5. State the need for Cath Lab activation.
