# NBME / USMLE Clinical Vignette Style Guide

## 1. Vignette Structure (The Stem)
Every clinical vignette must follow a strict, standardized chronological order:
1. **Patient Demographics & Chief Complaint:** Age, sex, and primary reason for visit (e.g., "A 45-year-old man comes to the emergency department because of a 2-hour history of severe chest pain.").
2. **History of Present Illness (HPI):** Chronological story, pain characteristics (radiating, sharp, dull), alleviating/aggravating factors.
3. **Past Medical History & Medications:** Only include relevant history, but add common distractors (HTN, Type 2 DM).
4. **Social & Family History:** Alcohol, smoking (in pack-years), illicit drugs, occupation.
5. **Physical Examination:**
   - Always start with Vitals: Temperature, Blood Pressure (BP), Pulse (HR), Respirations (RR), Oxygen saturation (SpO2). 
   - Group by system (General, HEENT, CV, Pulm, Abd, Neuro, Skin).
   - Use objective, descriptive findings (e.g., "A harsh crescendo-decrescendo systolic murmur is heard at the right upper sternal border" instead of "Aortic stenosis murmur").
6. **Diagnostic Studies:** Relevant Labs, ECG, or Imaging. Use standard SI or US units appropriately.

## 2. The Question Lead-in
End the stem with a direct, single-sentence question. Do not use negative phrasing like "Which of the following is NOT true?". 
* Examples: "Which of the following is the most likely diagnosis?", "What is the most appropriate next step in management?", "Which of the following describes the underlying pathophysiology of this patient's condition?"

## 3. Answer Options (Distractors)
* Provide exactly 5 options (A, B, C, D, E).
* All options must be homogenous (e.g., all medications, all diagnoses, all lab findings).
* Options should be arranged alphabetically or chronologically.

## 4. Explanations
* **Correct Answer Justification:** Explain *why* the correct answer is right by referencing specific clues in the vignette.
* **Distractor Explanations:** Explain *why* each incorrect option (A, B, C, D, E) is wrong, pointing out the missing or contradictory clinical findings in the stem that eliminate them.

## 5. Anti-Patterns (NEVER DO THIS)
* Avoid eponyms when descriptive terms exist.
* NEVER use clinical buzzwords (e.g., do not say "machine-like murmur" or "cherry-red spot"; describe what it actually looks/sounds like). Let the diagnostic reasoning happen in the student's head.
